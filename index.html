Research

Research

If certain conditions are met, mechanical systems can exhibit the remarkable ability to self-synchronize. This thesis implements low-order models of two such systems. The first models describe the behavior of two coupled pendula attached through a rigid body, a system commonly known as Huygens' clocks. The two pendula are subject to excitation through an external moment applied by an escapement mechanism. There are two types of escapement mechanisms considered: Hamiltonian and Van der Pol escapement. Depending on initial conditions and escapement parameters, the system will fall into one of three post-transient states, which are classified according to the phase difference between the two pendula. These states are: in-phase synchronization, out-of-phase synchronization, or asynchronous motion. One objective of this thesis is to map sets of parameters and initial conditions to one of these three states. The second set of models considered here shows how two oscillators may be synchronized without a physical connection. The system again includes two pendula, but now the rigid body is removed. Information on the state of a primary pendulum is passed to the secondary pendulum in the form of changes to the escapement moment. The results suggest the feasibility of synchronizing remote mechanical oscillators uisng wireless signals.

Projects

Thermal Vacuum Shroud

The project concerns the Small Satellite Research Labaoratory. Part of ensuring that the satellites work correctly in LEO requires testing. As a result the lab was given requirements by NASA, Air Force, and the University Nanosatellite Project (UNP) to test the satellites. Part of which is heating and cooling the satellite in both atmospheric and vacuum environments. It started with modeling the vacuum chamber.

It started with coming up with a variety of designs that would both fit the chamber and fulfill the heating requirements, the challenge was creating enough heat to increase the temperature of the satellite through radiation in 10e-6 torr. The framed picture is the final design.

Below is the final manufactured product. The principles are four heating elements to heat the satellite on the y sides. A copper coil surrounding a copper cylinder is extruded to help catch radiated heat. For cooling, liquid nitrogen is pumped through the tubes. Image on left pictures the shroud with a 3D printed model of satellite.

Realistic Hand Rig
