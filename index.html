Projects

Thermal Vacuum Shroud

The project concerns the Small Satellite Research Labaoratory. Part of ensuring that the satellites work correctly in LEO requires testing. As a result the lab was given requirements by NASA, Air Force, and the University Nanosatellite Project (UNP) to test the satellites. Part of which is heating and cooling the satellite in both atmospheric and vacuum environments. It started with modeling the vacuum chamber.
It started with coming up with a variety of designs that would both fit the chamber and fulfill the heating requirements, the challenge was creating enough heat to increase the temperature of the satellite through radiation in 10e-6 torr. The framed picture is the final design.
Below is the final manufactured product. The principles are four heating elements to heat the satellite on the y sides. A copper coil surrounding a copper cylinder is extruded to help catch radiated heat. For cooling, liquid nitrogen is pumped through the tubes. Image on left pictures the shroud with a 3D printed model of satellite.

Realistic Hand Rig

I started and finished the project over the last summer. I saw an extremely realistic facial rig on Twitter when I wanted to create my own. Below is a quick animation from the rig in Blender.
I took pictures of my hand in top and side perspectives and then used photoshop to level them out to give the right aspect rations for the two angles.

I used both Blender (right) and Autodesk 3ds Max (left).

Blender: Created textures, the texture is actually multi-layered meaning it has different layers through which the light penetrates, giving it a more realistic "skin-like" look. I also created the bone structure (vertex assignment), lighting and animation with this software.

3ds Max: Layed out my reference picutes as stated above. Then I created and positioned the mesh with respect to pictures.

Ongoing Project

  • I am working on stabilizing a drone using with the help of a neural network. So far I have set up a gyro and am working on firstly setting up the neural network in the gym environment.
